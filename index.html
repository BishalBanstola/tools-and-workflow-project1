Welcome to the Canadian Study Permit Guide

Explore the process and requirements for applying for a study permit in Canada. To apply for a study permit in Canada, individuals typically need to obtain an acceptance letter from a Canadian designated learning institution, demonstrate proof of financial support, provide a valid passport, and undergo a medical examination, among other requirements. Additionally, applicants may be required to submit a study plan and meet language proficiency criteria.

Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for a Canadian study permit, you must meet the following criteria:

  • Have an acceptance letter from a designated learning institution in Canada.
  • Prove that you have enough money to pay for your tuition fees, living expenses, and return transportation.
  • Have a clean criminal record and be willing to provide a police certificate.
  • Be in good health and willing to complete a medical examination if required.

Documents Required

Ensure you have the following documents for your study permit application:

  • Valid passport
  • Acceptance letter from a designated learning institution
  • Proof of financial support
  • Medical Certificate
  • Academic Transcripts and Certificates
  • English Language Proficiency Test Scores
  • Statement of Purpose
  • Resume
  • Police Clearance Certificate
